## Definition (Plain Language)

**Kill Confirms** means reliable setups that lead to stocks at realistic percents and spacing. In normal matches, it is less about theory and more about whether your decisions stay stable when pace and pressure increase.

## Why It Matters Competitively

They reduce desperation options in close games. Players who apply Kill confirms consistently usually lose fewer "free" stocks from panic decisions and convert more neutral openings into controlled advantage.

In tournament-style sets, Kill confirms matters even more because opponents adapt quickly. The player who can apply it under game-two and game-three pressure usually controls tempo.

## Common Beginner Misunderstanding

A frequent mistake is fishing raw kill moves instead of building confirm situations. That usually creates predictable patterns opponents can punish repeatedly.

Corrective mindset:

- Use Kill confirms to improve decision quality, not to force highlight plays.
- Pair it with positioning and habit tracking.
- Keep one low-risk default before adding advanced mixups.

## Practical In-Match Example

You land a safe starter at high percent and convert into a practiced closeout route.

A useful review prompt after each set: "Did Kill confirms create position and consistency, or did I use it too early and lose control?"

## What To Practice

Practice one stable kill confirm and one ledge closeout sequence. Build a short drill around it and tie success to match transfer, not just training-mode repetition.

Starter practice loop:

1. Pick one recurring scenario from replay review.
2. Run 10-20 deliberate reps with a clear success condition.
3. Test it in live matches and note one adaptation for next session.


Concrete checkpoint: in your next three games, call out one moment where **Kill confirms** appears and confirm whether your decision improved positioning.
