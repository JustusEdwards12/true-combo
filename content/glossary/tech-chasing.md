## Definition (Plain Language)

**Tech-Chasing** means covering knockdown options after a hit to continue pressure. In normal matches, it is less about theory and more about whether your decisions stay stable when pace and pressure increase.

## Why It Matters Competitively

It turns knockdowns into repeatable advantage rather than guesswork. Players who apply Tech-chasing consistently usually lose fewer "free" stocks from panic decisions and convert more neutral openings into controlled advantage.

In tournament-style sets, Tech-chasing matters even more because opponents adapt quickly. The player who can apply it under game-two and game-three pressure usually controls tempo.

## Common Beginner Misunderstanding

A frequent mistake is hard-reading every tech option immediately. That usually creates predictable patterns opponents can punish repeatedly.

Corrective mindset:

- Use Tech-chasing to improve decision quality, not to force highlight plays.
- Pair it with positioning and habit tracking.
- Keep one low-risk default before adding advanced mixups.

## Practical In-Match Example

You punish tech-in-place twice, then catch roll-out once opponent adapts.

A useful review prompt after each set: "Did Tech-chasing create position and consistency, or did I use it too early and lose control?"

## What To Practice

Practice covering two options first, then adapting on repetition. Build a short drill around it and tie success to match transfer, not just training-mode repetition.

Starter practice loop:

1. Pick one recurring scenario from replay review.
2. Run 10-20 deliberate reps with a clear success condition.
3. Test it in live matches and note one adaptation for next session.


Concrete checkpoint: in your next three games, call out one moment where **Tech-chasing** appears and confirm whether your decision improved positioning.
