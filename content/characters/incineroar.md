## Character overview

Incineroar is a slow but explosive grappler who punishes panic habits with command-grab mindgames and high damage.

## Difficulty level

Beginner-friendly

## Playstyle identity

Walk opponents toward corner, threaten command grab, and cash out with devastating close-range punishes.

## Strengths

- Huge reward from close openings
- Revenge amplifies punish value
- Strong ledge kill pressure

## Weaknesses

- Poor mobility in neutral
- Can be kited heavily
- Recovery is exploitable and linear

## Best beginner gameplan

- Use grounded patience to enter safely
- Condition shield then mix grab and strike
- Maintain corner pressure for stock closes

## Neutral strategy

- Walk-shield to claim space without dashing recklessly
- Use low-commit pokes to force reactions
- Avoid obvious side-B attempts

## Advantage state

- At ledge, threaten both command grab and anti-jump
- Punish panic with high-reward aerials
- Keep pressure simple and repeatable

## Disadvantage state

- Do not mash revenge under pressure
- Mix landing timings and drift
- Prioritize stage reset over reversals

## Recovery tips

- Vary up-B timing and drift
- Recover low to avoid easy intercepts
- Save jump for final route mix

## Common beginner mistakes

- Spamming side-B in neutral
- Fishing revenge at unsafe moments
- Predictable recoveries

## Key moves

- Alolan Whip: command-grab threat
- Revenge: punish amplifier
- Nair: close pressure
- Back air: kill option

## Out of shield options

- Up-B out of shield punish
- Nair out of shield in scramble
- Shield grab if pressure lands too close

## Basic combos

- Down throw into aerial pressure
- Nair starter into corner carry
- Revenge hit into simple finisher

## Kill confirms

- Back air at ledge
- Command-grab reads
- Up smash anti-air punish

## Matchup considerations

- Against zoners, patience is mandatory
- Against rushdown, punish overextensions hard
- Against heavies, leverage command-grab pressure

## Practical gameplay advice

- Incineroar wins by forcing fear at close range
- Do not rush neutral entries
- Use revenge as read, not reflex

## Training priorities

- Entry timing drills
- Command-grab mix practice
- Recovery survival reps
