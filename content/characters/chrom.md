## Character overview

Chrom is a straightforward sword pressure character with strong consistency and honest punish flow.

## Difficulty level

Beginner-friendly

## Playstyle identity

Control mid-range with disjoint pressure, then convert openings with stable routes and corner traps.

## Strengths

- Consistent sword damage across hitboxes
- Reliable pressure and whiff punishes
- Simple, effective conversion game

## Weaknesses

- Recovery is vulnerable and predictable
- Can be outmaneuvered by high mobility
- Needs good spacing to avoid scrambles

## Best beginner gameplan

- Hold sword range and force entries
- Punish unsafe landings with quick sword buttons
- Close stocks through ledge pressure

## Neutral strategy

- Use falling aerials at safe spacing
- Do not drift into shield at point-blank range
- Respect burst options before swinging

## Advantage state

- Juggle with up air and anti-air tilts
- At ledge, trap jump and neutral getup simply
- Keep center stage after each hit

## Disadvantage state

- Avoid panic counter usage
- Mix landing routes with platforms
- Save resources for recovery mixups

## Recovery tips

- Vary up-B timing and side positioning
- Recover low when possible
- Avoid repeating immediate ledge snaps

## Common beginner mistakes

- Overusing forward smash in neutral
- Landing in front of shield repeatedly
- Predictable recovery routes

## Key moves

- Nair: neutral pressure
- Up air: juggle core
- F-tilt: spacing check
- Back air: kill pressure

## Out of shield options

- Up-B out of shield hard punish
- Nair out of shield in close range
- Jump reset when pressure is safe

## Basic combos

- Nair starter into aerial pressure
- Up throw into juggle state
- Jab pressure into ledge carry

## Kill confirms

- Back air at ledge
- Up-B out of shield punish
- F-smash hard read

## Matchup considerations

- Against zoners, inch in with sword spacing
- Against rushdown, use disjoint anti-air timing
- Against heavies, hold center and trap exits

## Practical gameplay advice

- Chrom rewards consistency and spacing honesty
- Do not force high-risk reads when ahead
- Keep recovery routes varied every stock

## Training priorities

- Safe aerial spacing reps
- Ledge trap drills
- Recovery mixup practice
