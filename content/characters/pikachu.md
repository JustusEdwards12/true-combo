## Character overview

Pikachu is a high-mobility pressure character with tiny hurtbox interactions and relentless advantage looping.

## Difficulty level

Intermediate

## Playstyle identity

Harass with quick movement and safe aerial pressure, then snowball advantage with edgeguards and juggles.

## Strengths

- Small hurtbox creates whiff opportunities
- Excellent edgeguarding across many matchups
- Strong combo flow from low-commit starters

## Weaknesses

- Precise spacing and confirms require reps
- Can struggle to kill without setup against patient defense
- Light weight punishes mistakes hard

## Best beginner gameplan

- Start with safe aerial pokes and movement feints
- Convert hits into offstage pressure rather than raw kill swings
- Keep opponents in corner to force panic

## Neutral strategy

- Threaten quick aerial drift-in but fade back often
- Use thunder jolt to force shield or jump decisions
- Stay aware of opponent anti-air burst range

## Advantage state

- Prioritize edgeguards over center-stage hard reads
- Loop juggle pressure with up air timing
- Use ledge pressure to bait jump and airdodge

## Disadvantage state

- Avoid predictable panic thunder attempts
- Mix landing with drift and quick aerial stalls
- Do not spend all mobility resources early

## Recovery tips

- Mix quick attack angles each stock
- Recover from below ledge to reduce intercept risk
- Save second quick attack angle for adaptation

## Common beginner mistakes

- Overforcing kill confirms at mid percent
- Spamming approach aerials without conditioning
- Recovering with the same quick attack route

## Key moves

- Thunder Jolt: neutral entry tool
- Nair: combo starter and pressure
- Back air: edgeguard staple
- Quick Attack: mobility and recovery

## Out of shield options

- Nair out of shield in close pressure
- Up smash as hard punish on unsafe landings
- Jump reset when shield pressure is too safe

## Basic combos

- Nair or up tilt into aerial strings
- Down throw into platform chase routes
- Edge hit into back air edgeguard continuation

## Kill confirms

- Edgeguard back air chains
- Thunder read on vertical recovery
- Up smash punish on whiffed landings

## Matchup considerations

- Against swordies, prioritize hurtbox weaving and whiff punishes
- Against heavies, maximize combo extensions
- Against zoners, use low profile movement plus thunder jolt pressure

## Practical gameplay advice

- Pikachu thrives on controlled aggression
- Keep your tempo varied so defense cannot settle
- Force adaptation through repeated edge pressure

## Training priorities

- Quick attack angle consistency
- Edgeguard route practice
- Nair starter conversion drills
