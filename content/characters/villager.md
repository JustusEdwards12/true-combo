## Character overview

Villager is a trap-zoning character with strong slingshot control and anti-approach tools.

## Difficulty level

Intermediate

## Playstyle identity

Set tempo with projectiles and stage objects, then punish impatience near ledge and platforms.

## Strengths

- Excellent lane control with slingshot
- Strong ledge harassment
- Can force awkward approach timings

## Weaknesses

- Can struggle in sustained close scrambles
- Setup habits become punishable if repetitive
- Kill confirms need planning

## Best beginner gameplan

- Use slingshot to hold burst range
- Plant trap pressure in key movement lanes
- Close stocks through ledge and anti-air reads

## Neutral strategy

- Rotate projectile rhythm to avoid autopilot
- Do not overcommit to tree setup in unsafe spots
- Walk opponents to corner with chip pressure

## Advantage state

- At ledge, force jumps with projectile presence
- Use anti-air swings on panic landings
- Maintain space control instead of overchasing

## Disadvantage state

- Avoid panic aerials when cornered
- Mix drift and resource use to land
- Reset spacing with movement first

## Recovery tips

- Vary balloon recovery heights
- Recover around edgeguard hitboxes
- Do not repeat same ledge timing

## Common beginner mistakes

- Slingshot spam with no adaptation
- Unsafe tree commitments
- Predictable recovery heights

## Key moves

- Slingshot: neutral control
- Lloid Rocket: lane disruption
- Fair/Bair: spacing pressure
- Up tilt: anti-air

## Out of shield options

- Nair out of shield in scramble range
- Grab out of shield against close pressure
- Jump reset when options are safely spaced

## Basic combos

- Projectile hit into aerial follow-up
- Nair pressure into corner carry
- Trap setup into anti-air punish

## Kill confirms

- Ledge trap projectile into finish
- Back air corner callout
- F-smash hard read after conditioning

## Matchup considerations

- Against rushdown, maintain lane denial discipline
- Against zoners, contest with mixed projectile timings
- Against heavies, keep chip and ledge pressure

## Practical gameplay advice

- Villager wins through irritation and control
- Do not abandon setup identity in scrambles
- Adapt projectile lanes as sets progress

## Training priorities

- Slingshot rhythm variation
- Ledge trap route drills
- Recovery height mixing
