## Character overview

Sonic is a mobility control character who wins through burst threat, whiff punishes, and tempo management.

## Difficulty level

Intermediate

## Playstyle identity

Use speed to force reactions, then punish habits while minimizing unsafe commitments.

## Strengths

- Top-tier movement and burst range
- Excellent hit-and-run neutral
- Strong ability to force impatient mistakes

## Weaknesses

- Can struggle to close stocks if overpassive
- Predictable spin rhythms get punished
- Damage output can be low without confirms

## Best beginner gameplan

- Scout defensive habits with movement feints
- Take guaranteed punishes from whiffs
- Close games with corner pressure and confirms

## Neutral strategy

- Dash around burst range and force overreactions
- Do not spin from obvious timings
- Use grounded patience to bait anti-spin options

## Advantage state

- Push opponents to ledge and trap jumps
- Use up air strings when anti-air starter connects
- Keep pressure safe to maintain lead

## Disadvantage state

- Avoid panic spin from disadvantage
- Mix drift and delayed landings
- Use speed to reset stage control quickly

## Recovery tips

- Mix spring timing and drift path
- Recover low when anti-air traps are active
- Use side-B recovery routes sparingly

## Common beginner mistakes

- Overusing spin dash entry from same distance
- Playing too passive when kill is needed
- Ignoring ledge pressure opportunities

## Key moves

- Spin Dash: burst conditioning tool
- Back air: spacing and kill option
- Up air: juggle pressure
- Homing Attack: situational callout

## Out of shield options

- Nair out of shield in close pressure
- Up smash punish on unsafe jumps
- Jump reset versus safe shield pressure

## Basic combos

- Spin hit into aerial follow-up
- Up throw into juggle pressure
- Nair starter into ledge carry

## Kill confirms

- Back air edge pressure
- Up smash anti-air punish
- Ledge trap callout into forward smash

## Matchup considerations

- Against zoners, exploit movement to break setup
- Against swordies, punish laggy spacing attempts
- Against heavies, take low-risk chip and ledge control

## Practical gameplay advice

- Sonic wins by forcing frustration
- Keep adaptation ahead of opponent anti-spin plans
- Stage control still matters even with speed

## Training priorities

- Burst-stop spacing drills
- Whiff punish reaction practice
- Ledge trap consistency
