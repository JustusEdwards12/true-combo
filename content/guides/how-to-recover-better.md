## Intro

Better recovery is route discipline under pressure. You survive longer by varying timing and spacing, not by gambling one panic option.

## Practical Examples

- Recover low once, then mix high drift to break edgeguard timing.
- Save double jump until opponent commits to edgeguard path.
- Use stage tech awareness when forced into wall routes.

## Common Mistakes

- Burning jump before reading edgeguard commitment.
- Recovering the same angle every stock.
- Directional airdodge to ledge without conditioning.

## Focus First

Build a recovery tree with at least three branches: low, high, and delayed snap route.

## In-Match Adjustments

- If opponent covers low, threaten high drift and land safely.
- If they chase high, fake height and snap ledge late.
- If ledge trap is strong, vary ledge timing before getup.

## Quick Tips

- Recovery is information warfare.
- Keep one resource unspent whenever possible.
- Plan route before you leave stage.
