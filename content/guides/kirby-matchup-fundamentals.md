## Intro

Kirby matchups often become tempo checks around jump drift and close-range burst. Respect Kirby's punish range while exploiting movement limitations.

## Practical Examples

- Against Kirby, hold disjoint range and punish grounded commitments.
- As Kirby, force shield fear with aerial drift then tomahawk options.
- At ledge, watch for jump habit and punish re-entry timing.

## Common Mistakes

- Challenging Kirby in point-blank scramble without plan.
- Letting Kirby recover uncontested when resources are obvious.
- As Kirby, always approaching with same jump arc.

## Focus First

Focus on anti-jump coverage and corner escapes first; both determine most Kirby stocks.

## In-Match Adjustments

- If Kirby crouches often, use spacing tools that bypass low profile risk.
- If opponents outrange Kirby, prioritize whiff punish traps.
- If both players are scrapping, slow tempo and retake stage.

## Quick Tips

- Kirby rewards patience and reads.
- Do not underestimate ledge threat in this matchup.
- Positioning solves more than raw aggression.
