## Intro

Directional Influence changes your launch angle after getting hit. Good DI is matchup-aware and percent-aware, not random stick movement.

## Practical Examples

- Against horizontal kill throws, DI out to increase distance from blast zone.
- Against juggle starters, DI to force awkward follow-up angles.
- In training mode, save common kill setups and test DI responses.

## Common Mistakes

- Always holding in regardless of move angle.
- Reacting too late and only mashing stick after launch.
- Forgetting to combine DI with jump/resource planning.

## Focus First

Build a DI chart for three opponents you face often and review it weekly.

## In-Match Adjustments

- If you die early to same move, test different DI lanes immediately.
- If comboed by up airs, DI to platforms when possible.
- If cornered at high percent, prioritize survival DI over reversal attempts.

## Quick Tips

- DI buys chances, not guaranteed escapes.
- Know your character weight and drift options.
- Use replay pause to inspect launch angles.
